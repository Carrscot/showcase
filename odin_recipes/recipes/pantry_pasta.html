Pantry Pasta

Description

This recipe is based off of what is traditionally know as "Spaghetti aglio olio". However, it can be replicated with any fresh herbs or ingredients you have in the kitchen so long as you have pasta and olive oil. It is deceptively easy to make and overperforms when it comes to flavour.

If you are short on time or ingredients this is the perfect recipe as this version contains only 5 ingredients and takes about 15 to 20 minutes to prepare. The secret is in the quality of the ingredients and the preparation!

Ingredients

  • 200g of Any pasta - tagliatelle for this recipe
  • Good olive oil
  • 2 Cloves of garlic
  • Chili Flakes
  • A large bunch of fresh parsley

Preparation Instructions

  1. Thinly slice the garlic. Try get the slices to be as thin as possible.
  2. Finely chop the fresh parsley
  3. Boil an appraopriately sized pot of water, remember to add a significant amount of salt to your water. Once the water is boiling add your pasta and preapre as per instructions on the packaging.
  4. While the water is boiling place a large amount of olive oil in a pan on medium heat. The olive oil should be about a half a centimeter deep.
  5. When you have put your pasta in and your oil has heated up pour some chili flakes into the oil. Depending on your tolerance for spice you can add more or less to your taste.
  6. When the pasta is 1 minute away from being cooked el dente add the garlic to the oil. Once the garlic starts to get some colour and becomes fragrant it is ready. If your garlic becomes to brown it will become bitter so try to watch it as best as you can.
  7. Once the pasta is cooked el dente turn the heat off straight away and bring your pot close to your pan.
  8. Transfer the pasta straight from the pot into the pan. Do not worry about getting some pasta water in the pan, this is a good thing!
  9. Add a quater cup of your pasta water into the pan and turn the heat down to medium low. Stir all the ingredients together for a minute or until the olive oil and pasta water have emuslified properly.
  10. Add your finely chopped parsley to the pot reserving some for garnish.
  11. Plate as desired and enjoy!

This recipe comes together pretty quickly so make sure that everything is ready to go once you start. This recipe is great to enjoy on your own or with friends all you have to do is scale up the ingredients accordingly.
